SpaceX strikes $17B deal to buy EchoStar’s spectrum for Starlink’s direct-to-phone service

2025-09-08T13:26:57+00:00
  • On August 26, EchoStar sold $23 billion worth of spectrum licenses to AT&T.
  • Of the cash, $2 billion will be made as direct cash interest payments on debt held by EchoStar.
  • EchoStar will sell its AWS-4 and H-block spectrum licenses in exchange for $8.5 billion in cash and $8.5 billion in SpaceX stock.
  • As part of the move, EchoStar simultaneously canceled a $1.3 billion contract with Canadian satellite maker MDA Space that was announced just five weeks earlier in August for 100 satellites.
  • SpaceX said Monday that the new spectrum will enable upgraded satellites with “more than 100 times” the capacity of first-generation Starlink direct-to-cell satellites.
  • Elon Musk’s SpaceX has agreed to acquire 50 MHz of wireless spectrum and mobile satellite service spectrum licenses from EchoStar for use in the Starlink satellite network.
  • The deal also gives customers of EchoStar’s Boost Mobile service access to Starlink’s direct-to-phone service.
