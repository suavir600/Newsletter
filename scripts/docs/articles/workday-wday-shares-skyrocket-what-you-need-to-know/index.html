Workday (WDAY) Shares Skyrocket, What You Need To Know

2025-09-17T17:36:38+00:00
  • Workday is down 6.9% since the beginning of the year, and at $234.56 per share, it is trading 16.2% below its 52-week high of $279.91 from December 2024.
  • The finance and HR software provider reported calendar second-quarter revenue of $2.35 billion, up 12.6% year over year, which was in line with Wall Street's expectations.
  • Shares of enterprise software company Workday (NASDAQ:WDAY) jumped 8% in the morning session after activist investor Elliott Management disclosed a more than $2 billion stake and praised the company's leadership.
  • Adjusted earnings per share came in at $2.21, beating the consensus estimate of $2.12.
  • Workday’s shares are not very volatile and have only had 7 moves greater than 5% over the last year.
  • Investors may have also been concerned about slowing growth, as the 12.6% revenue increase represents a deceleration from the company's 16.4% compounded annual growth rate over the last three years.
  • The previous big move we wrote about was 26 days ago when the stock dropped 3.6% on the news that the company reported second-quarter results that, while beating profit estimates, failed to impress on the top line.
