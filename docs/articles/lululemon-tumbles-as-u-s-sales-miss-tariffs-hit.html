Lululemon Tumbles as U.S. Sales Miss, Tariffs Hit

  • Revenue climbed 6.8% to $2.5 billion, about $40 million shy of expectations, while earnings of $3.10 a share topped forecasts by $0.25 but dipped slightly from a year ago.
  • Tariffs alone are expected to trim $240 million from gross profit.
  • Gross margin slipped 110 basis points to 58.5%, still ahead of consensus.
  • Comparable sales inched up 1%, missing estimates, as stronger traffic in China wasn't enough to offset softness in the Americas.
  • Lululemon now expects FY25 revenue of $10.85 billion$11 billion, below Wall Street's $11.2 billion call, and profit of $12.77$12.97 a share, well under its prior outlook near $14.70.
  • Q3 guidance also disappointed, with sales and earnings projected below analyst targets.
  • Looking ahead, the picture darkened.
