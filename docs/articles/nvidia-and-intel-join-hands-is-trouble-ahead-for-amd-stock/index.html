Nvidia and Intel Join Hands. Is Trouble Ahead for AMD Stock?

2025-09-19T15:39:48+00:00
  • Overall revenue rose 32% year-over-year to $7.7 billion, exceeding expectations.
  • AMD stock has already been under pressure, down roughly 15.3% from its recent 52-week high of $186.65.
  • The data center segment delivered $3.2 billion in sales, up 14% despite weakness in AI revenue, driven by continued strength in EPYC processors across cloud and enterprise workloads.
  • The company entered the second half of 2025 with multiple catalysts that could reaccelerate its growth.
  • While the development led to a 22.8% rally in Intel stock yesterday, it could bring trouble for AMD as two of its biggest competitors are now working together.
  • To strengthen the partnership, Nvidia will also invest $5 billion in Intel stock at $23.28 per share, which will require regulatory approval.
  • EPYC has now posted 33 straight quarters of market share gains.
