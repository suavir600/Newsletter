JPMorgan Chase's (NYSE:JPM) Shareholders Will Receive A Bigger Dividend Than Last Year

2025-09-20T13:17:15+00:00
  • Since 2015, the dividend has gone from $1.60 total annually to $5.60.
  • Looking forward, EPS is forecast to rise by 12.5% over the next 3 years.
  • Even though the dividend went up, the yield is still quite low at only 1.8%.
  • This implies that the company grew its distributions at a yearly rate of about 13% over that duration.
  • Analysts forecast the future payout ratio could be 31% over the same time horizon, which is a number we think the company can maintain.
  • It's encouraging to see that JPMorgan Chase has been growing its earnings per share at 22% a year over the past five years.
  • Past distributions do not necessarily guarantee future ones, but JPMorgan Chase's payout ratio of 27% is a good sign as this means that earnings decently cover dividends.
