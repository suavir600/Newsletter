Smaller Discounts and Earlier Sales: What to Expect this Holiday Season

2025-09-20T09:00:00+00:00
  • Almost 40% anticipate they’ll begin their holiday shopping by October, according to consulting firm Simon-Kucher.
  • This year, Americans are expected to spend $1.6 trillion during the period, according to consulting firm Deloitte.
  • About 75% of retailers surveyed in May indicated that they would reduce the size or scope of discounts because of tariffs, Signifyd said.
  • About 40% of retail sales occur in the fourth quarter, said Shikha Jain, partner for consumer and retail in North America at Simon-Kucher.
  • Nearly 26% of products were on sale, compared to roughly 24% last summer, he said, while the average discount was almost 3 percentage points smaller.
  • Promotions may start sooner this year, and some 40% of surveyed consumers plan to start shopping by October, according to Simon-Kucher, a consulting firm.
  • The thrifty ethos may spill into the holiday season, with people planning to spend an average of 5% less than last year, according to consulting firm PWC.
