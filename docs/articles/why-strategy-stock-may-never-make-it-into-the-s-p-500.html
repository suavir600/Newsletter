Why Strategy Stock May Never Make It Into the S&P 500

  • Strategy missed the cut for admission into the S&P 500 Friday when three additions were announced— AppLovin Robinhood Markets and Emcor —and it could be tough for the big Bitcoin holder to be chosen.
  • The company looks more like an exchange-traded fund or closed-end fund than an operating company with ample recurring revenue and profits.
  • The company’s strategy is to issue equity—mostly common stock and some preferred—and buy Bitcoin, making it a giant repository of the cryptocurrency.
