Lululemon's Stock Continues to Be a Lemon. Can the Stock Rebound, or Should Investors Stay Away?

2025-09-08 07:32:00+00:00
  • Americas' revenue edged up 1% to $1.8 billion, while same-store sales fell 4%.
  • Rest-of-world sales jumped 19% to $374.1 million, as comparable-store sales climbed 12%.
  • China led the way, with revenue roaring 25% higher to $392.9 million, with same-store sales up 17%.
  • Overall revenue climbed by 7% year over year to $2.53 billion, while its adjusted earnings per share (EPS) fell by less than 2% to $3.10.
  • For fiscal Q3, Lululemon is looking for sales of between $2.47 billion and $2.5 billion, good for growth of 3% to 4%, and adjusted EPS of between $2.18 and $2.23.
  • However, international revenue climbed 22%, with comparable-store sales surging 15%.
  • Lululemon's men's category saw revenue rise by 6%, while women's sales increased by 5%.
