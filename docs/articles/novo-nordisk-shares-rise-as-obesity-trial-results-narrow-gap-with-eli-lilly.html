Novo Nordisk Shares Rise as Obesity Trial Results Narrow Gap With Eli Lilly

  • Sep 15 - Novo Nordisk (NYSE:NVO) reported that a higher semaglutide dose helped patients lose more weight in two late-stage trials, and its Copenhagen-listed shares rose about 1.5%.
  • The trials tested a 7.2 mg dose, three times the approved 2.4 mg, and measured weight change over 72 weeks.

  • This article first appeared on GuruFocus.
  • In the larger study, participants on 7.2 mg lost an average of 19 percent of body weight, compared with about 16 percent on 2.4 mg and 4 percent on placebo.
  • Novo has faced setbacks in the U.S., including market share losses and a roughly 60 percent share decline over the past year as it trims its global workforce.
  • The data may strengthen Novo's case for higher-dose strategies if regulators and payers accept the approach.
