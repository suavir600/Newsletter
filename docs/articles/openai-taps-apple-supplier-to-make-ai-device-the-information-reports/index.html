OpenAI taps Apple supplier to make AI device, the Information reports

2025-09-19T13:30:33+00:00
  • OpenAI bought hardware startup io Products, founded by former Apple designer Jony Ive, earlier this year in a $6.5 billion deal, underscoring its push to move beyond software into consumer hardware.
  • (Reporting by Akash Sriram in Bengaluru; Editing by Shilpi Majumdar)
  • Luxshare and OpenAI did not immediately respond to Reuters' requests for comment.
  • The goal is to create an "AI-native" product, hardware designed from the ground up to work with AI models, rather than bolted onto existing platforms.
  • (Reuters) - OpenAI ( OPAI.PVT ) has signed a deal with Apple ( AAPL ) device assembler, Luxshare ( 002475.SZ ), to make a consumer device, the Information reported on Friday, citing people familiar with the matter.
  • OpenAI has also approached China-based Goertek ( 002241.SZ ), a supplier that assembles AirPods, HomePods and Apple Watches, to provide components, including speaker modules, for its planned devices, the Information report said.
  • Luxshare, a major assembler of Apple's iPhones and AirPods, would bring large-scale manufacturing capabilities to OpenAI's effort.
