Lyft stock surges 12% on Waymo robotaxi deal in Nashville

2025-09-17T15:10:22+00:00
  • Uber shares were down about 5% following the announcement on Wednesday morning.
  • Uber's market cap, however, is $195 billion, dwarfing Lyft's $9 billion valuation.
  • It could be worth more than $330 billion by 2030, with robotaxi-type services pushing the industry forward and reducing costs by — among other factors —not requiring a human driver.
  • Even so, the firm "would like to see positive fundamental trends sustained over time to become constructive on the shares." Lyft stock is up 70% year to date versus Uber's 48% rise during the same period.
  • Riders can initially hail driverless cars through the Waymo app, with plans to integrate the fleet into Lyft’s platform later in 2026.
  • Goldman analyst Mark Delaney estimates the market for traditional ride-hailing in the US is worth approximately $58 billion currently.
  • Lyft ( LYFT ) stock surged 13% Wednesday after the company announced a partnership with Alphabet’s ( GOOG ) self-driving Waymo service to roll out an autonomous ride-hailing service in Nashville next year.
