HPE Fires Off $2.5B Bond Blitz to Fuel Juniper Play in Debt Market Frenzy

  • That comes on the heels of a blockbuster $67 billion issuance week and a projected $45$50 billion this week.
  • Hewlett Packard Enterprise (NYSE:HPE) is stepping into the bond market with a $2.5 billion multi-tranche offering as it works to finance its recent acquisition of Juniper Networks.
  • This article first appeared on GuruFocus.
  • This deal could mark a quiet inflection point for HPE's balance sheet strategy.
  • Their timing might not be coincidentalHPE's deal lands amid a surge of corporate issuance, with about 17 companies tapping the market on Monday alone.
  • According to people familiar with the matter, the four-part deal includes both fixed and floating-rate notes, with the longest five-year tranche initially guided at roughly 110 basis points over Treasuries.
  • With the Juniper acquisition in play, HPE appears to be leaning into a more network-centric future, one potentially more recurring and software-driven.
