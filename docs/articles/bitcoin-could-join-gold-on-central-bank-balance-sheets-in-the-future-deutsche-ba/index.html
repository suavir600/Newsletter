Bitcoin could join gold on central bank balance sheets in the future, Deutsche Bank says

2025-09-22T19:11:49+00:00
  • A 2025 World Council survey cited in the report found that 43% of central banks expect to increase their gold reserves over the next 12 months, while 95% anticipate global central bank reserves will rise.
  • (Getty Images) · brightstars via Getty Images The report also framed a broader question for the next decade, particularly around how central bank reserves might evolve as the dollar's share continues to decline, falling from 60% in 2000 to 43% in 2024.
  • Even so, Deutsche Bank's note highlights that 2025 has been an "excellent" year for both assets.
  • That bullish outlook comes after bitcoin ( BTC-USD) fell sharply on Monday , slipping below $113,000 after briefly topping $123,500 in August.
  • Meanwhile, gold surged to a fresh all-time high of $3,703 per ounce, posting its biggest yearly gain in over four decades.
  • There is room for both gold and Bitcoin to coexist on central bank balance sheets by 2030," Deutsche Bank analysts Marion Laboure and Camilla Siazon wrote in a recent note.
