D.R. Horton Stock Rises 48% in 3 Months: Is This Homebuilder Still a Buy?

2025-09-08 15:08:00+00:00
  • Revenues are projected to fall 7.6% year over year in fiscal 2025 before growing 1.5% in 2026.
  • Importantly, management raised its fiscal 2025 share repurchase target to $4.2–$4.4 billion, having already bought back $3.6 billion year to date, reducing outstanding shares by 9%.
  • The revised figures still imply an 18% earnings decline in fiscal 2025, but a 2.5% rebound in 2026.
  • For fiscal 2026, analysts expect a modest rebound with 2.5% EPS growth and 1.5% revenue improvement, aided by stabilization in housing demand and easing cost pressures.
  • Horton Revenues and EPS for fiscal 2025 are projected to decline 7.6% and 18%, respectively, reflecting pressure from affordability constraints, higher incentive spending, and slower absorption rates.
  • Gross margins are expected to dip to 21–21.5% in the fiscal fourth quarter as incentives rise to stimulate traffic.
  • Meanwhile, permits for future single-family construction inched up 0.5% to 870,000 units, ending a four-month decline.
