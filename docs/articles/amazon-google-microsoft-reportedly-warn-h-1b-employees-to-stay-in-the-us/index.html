Amazon, Google, Microsoft reportedly warn H-1B employees to stay in the US

2025-09-20T19:01:44+00:00
  • The White House announced Friday that Trump had signed a proclamation requiring employers to pay a $100,000 fee for H-1B visa applications.
  • President Donald Trump speaks during an executive order signing ceremony in the Roosevelt Room of the White House on July 31, 2025 in Washington, DC.
  • WASHINGTON, DC - JULY 31: U.S.
  • TechCrunch has reached out to Amazon, Microsoft, and Google for comment.
  • Business Insider published the memos from Amazon and Microsoft , while Sources published a similar memo from Google .
  • In response, Amazon, Google, and Microsoft reportedly emailed their employees telling those with H-1B visas to stay in the United States and avoid foreign travel for now — and if they’re already traveling, to try to return before the proclamation takes effect at 12:01am Eastern on Sunday.
  • Meanwhile, a White House official told Axios that the fee will only apply to new applicants, not existing H-1B holders or renewals.
