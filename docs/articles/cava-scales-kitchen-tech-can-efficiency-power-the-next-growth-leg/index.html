CAVA Scales Kitchen Tech: Can Efficiency Power the Next Growth Leg?

2025-09-08 15:52:00+00:00 UTC
  • In second-quarter 2025, revenues increased 20.3% year over year to $278.2 million, while adjusted EBITDA rose 22.6% to $42.1 million.
  • CAVA is also scaling AI vision technology, extending real-time production monitoring to 21 additional restaurants in 2025.
  • At the center of this strategy is the rollout of kitchen display systems, which are expected to be live in 270 restaurants by 2025-end.
  • Restaurant-level margins held steady at 26.3%, supported by disciplined cost control despite modest 2.1% same-store sales growth and flat traffic.
  • Management expects these investments to reinforce Chipotle’s path back to mid-single-digit comp growth and sustain restaurant-level margins near the high-20% range.
  • Alongside this, the deployment of TurboChef ovens across the fleet by 2025 is designed to accelerate cooking times while ensuring quality consistency.
  • With $385.8 million in cash and no debt, CAVA maintains significant balance sheet flexibility to invest in pilots and systemwide rollouts without straining capital resources.
