Uber's $2 Billion Bond Play: Is Wall Street About to Bite?

2025-09-08 21:32:30+00:00 UTC
  • This move follows Uber's $4 billion raise roughly a year ago and arrives at a moment when conditions couldn't be more borrower-friendly.
  • Uber Technologies (NYSE:UBER) is heading back to the bond market with a fresh $2 billion investment-grade deal, its second since earning blue-chip status.
  • According to people familiar with the terms, the offering may be split into two tranches, with one extending as far as 10 years.
  • This article first appeared on GuruFocus.
  • While management hasn't provided forward guidance tied to the issuance, the timing suggests Uber sees a window worth locking in.
  • Proceeds are slated for general corporate use, and the lead managersBarclays, Goldman Sachs, and JPMorganare lining up investor interest.
  • Early whispers put pricing on the longer note around 110 basis points above Treasuriesan attractive spread in a market where yield hunger is outpacing new issuance.
